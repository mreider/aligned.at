Example Analysis

StreamReach

Survey results and interpretation

Respondents

Fifty-six respondents completed the questionnaire, which took an average of twenty-six minutes to finish. The majority of the participants work in sales and are based in North America. However, there was also significant representation from other teams and regions. This diverse participation helps to ensure that the survey captures a comprehensive view of the team's consensus on the product's positioning, strengths, weaknesses, and the strategic direction necessary for the company's success

Value to customers

The results indicate a division in perceptions of StreamReach's strongest value offered to customers. The most frequently cited advantage was the ease of instrumentation, closely followed by the weekly industry reports. This contrasts with insights from customer and executive interviews, suggesting a discrepancy between employee beliefs and the founders' views. Notably, the comprehensive reports delivered to ad agencies were among the least valued features, implying that sales and support teams, which made up the majority of respondents, may prioritize certain aspects of the product over others when communicating its value.

Value to customers

The majority of respondents identified the lack of YouTube player instrumentation as the primary obstacle to customer adoption of the product. This finding is intriguing as it contrasts with responses to a subsequent question about product enhancements. Additionally, technical constraints make this feature virtually impossible to implement, posing a significant challenge for StreamReach. The prominence of a feature that cannot be delivered indicates a need for the company to reposition the product both internally and externally.

Competitive risks

Most participants felt that an ad agency might opt for a competitor's product due to better relationships, a stronger track record, or lack of awareness of StreamReach’s solutions. This perspective contrasts sharply with the executive team's view of the competition and their emphasis on "white glove" treatment during proofs of concept. This discrepancy raises concerns about whether the field sales team is adequately supported by effective marketing, a concern echoed in discussions about challenges in lead generation and filling the top of the sales funnel.

Strongest competitor

This response is concerning because while the leadership team identified AdTrends as the major competitor, internal teams actually ranked this competitor below Engagement Base and Broadcast, and on par with Quantics. With many competitors in the market, it’s challenging to pinpoint what distinctly sets StreamReach apart. To truly differentiate, it may be necessary to listen more closely to market feedback and consider strategic investments in technology.

Head-to-head performance

Currently, the field team perceives StreamReach's performance in head-to-head competitive situations as challenging, aligning with the broader organizational sentiment. The difficulty varies depending on the competitive context. Against established vendors or YouTube's built-in analytics, securing a win is tough. However, when competing against newer rivals, StreamReach often finds it easier to win deals without resorting to price concessions.

Product Enhancements

The feedback indicates that enhancing YouTube analytics remains a top priority for product improvement. However, this poses a significant challenge for the engineering team, as direct YouTube player instrumentation is not feasible. Alternative analytics, like view counts from the YouTube API, are possible but do not meet the team's expectations. Consequently, the company needs to accept the difficult reality that it must find value in other areas and adjust its positioning accordingly. On a positive note, the social networking feature, which is currently prioritized by the engineering team, aligns well with what the company is promoting. This demonstrates a good sign of alignment between teams and leadership, suggesting that strategic initiatives are in sync across different segments of the organization.

Eval Inclusion

The company universally recognizes its frequency of inclusion in evaluations, although the survey results seem a bit unclear and might benefit from a clearer question structure. However, examining the text responses and corroborating with interviews, it's evident that inclusion typically hinges on the effectiveness of the lead generation team to funnel leads effectively. Generally, StreamReach is not included in evaluations without proactive efforts to secure a spot. There is a widespread acknowledgment that the company needs to enhance its market exposure, potentially through trade shows, speaking engagements, podcasts, and other public platforms. This recognition of weak exposure but understanding of its importance is a critical step toward addressing and improving market presence.

YouTube Analytics

The survey responses indicate a partial alignment; when respondents encounter customers using YouTube Analytics, they tend to see it as a sales opportunity rather than a competitive threat. This suggests some level of confidence in competing with YouTube, yet it also highlights a gap in recognizing the full competitive landscape, as acknowledged by the company's founders. StreamReach should consider this partial alignment as a cue to further refine its competitive strategy. While there is potential to offer unique value to YouTube users, the company might benefit from focusing on distinctive features outside of direct YouTube analytics to better differentiate itself in the market.
